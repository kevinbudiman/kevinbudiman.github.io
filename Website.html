A lot of Pramoedya Ananta Toer's books are for sale again in Gramedia now; most essential is the "Tetralogi Buru": Bumi Manusia, Anak Semua Bangsa, Jejak Langkah and Rumah Kaca. Bumi Manusia stands on its own if you don't want to dive into the whole tetralogy. Bukan Pasar Malam is a really nice early novella of his.


Here are some classics, all of which I personally recommend: Belenggu by Armijn Pane, Atheis by Achdiat K. Mihardja, Ziarah by Iwan Simatupang, Para Priyayi by Umar Kayam, and Tarian Bumi by Oka Rusmini. Eka Kurniawan's Cantik Itu Luka and Lelaki Harimau are on their way to classic status.


As for where to get them: I was in Gramedia a couple weeks ago and saw the Eka, the Oka, and the Pramoedya. Older literature is hit or miss; it will get reprinted, then fall out of print again, and then might appear later. You can find used copies of some things in Blok M Square in Jakarta but in my experience it's best to go there with a long list of possible authors and titles, since you never know what will be available.


Another option: if you have borrowing privileges at a major university library (think UI or UGM), you can borrow the book and then bring it to a print shop (like where students go to print assignments, jilid skripsi etc.) to make a reproduction. If you want to save money you can just have just a plain paper cover, but they can also produce professional-looking books. There are tons of these kinds of shops near campuses; you might have to try a few, since price and quality vary.


The canon if Indonedian literature has been pretty much established, from Raja Ali Hadji up to 2010-ish literary works. The ones already mentioned by others are some of the canon + a few overrated names. A popular book doesn't always mean it's good in terms of literary merit, but it's all about the mass' taste, the zeitgeist, etc.


A few names/books that deserve wider readership:
Essays: Goenawan Mohamad (the volumes of Catatan Pinggir from the 80's-90's are gold, the current ones are more of recycled thoughts and ideas), Hasif Amini, Nirwan Arsuka (on science, sociocultural, literature). Other non-fiction works about Indonesia are too broad to name.
Poetry: Chairil Anwar, Sapardi Djoko Damono, GM again, Afrizal Malna, Joko Pinurbo.
Literary fiction: The (not-too) oldies--any Iwan Simatupang, Budi Darma (Olenka), Umar Kayam (Kunang2 di Manhattan), Ahmad Tohari, Seno Gumira Adjidharma (Jazz parfum dan Insiden).
Excellent but underrated literary fictions: A.S. Laksana (a regressing fabulist), Nukila Amal (beautiful aphoristic writing, Inception-like), Linda Christanty (political and other shits humans do, clean prose), Azhari (his stories have a certain hikayat feel about them)




Cooking

Created Sunday 29 October 2023


Professional chef here,
Masakan Indonesia sebenernya banyak kok yang bumbunya sama. Basicnya cuma bawang merah, bawang putih, kunyit, jahe, lengkuas, cabe merah, ketumbar, daun salam, sereh, dan kemiri. Yang beda-bedain tinggal perbandingannya aja.
Mau bikin soto? bahan-bahan yang tadi disebut tinggal dialusin (kecuali sereh dan daun salam), goreng dengan minyak sedikit sampe wangi, tambah air + santan di akhir (optional)
Mau bikin rendang? goreng lagi bumbunya, tambah daun kunyit (optional), tambah daging sampe kecoklatan, tambah santen yang banyak, tunggu deh sampe kering.
Mau bikin rica-rica? Tinggal cabainya dibanyakin + tambah tomat.
Makanan Indo emang butuh banyak herbs and spices, keliatannya ribet, tapi method of cookingnya gitu-gitu doang sebenernya.
Edit: Sedikit acidity juga ngebantu nambahin rasa gurih di masakan. Coba tambahin sedikit air asam jawa atau perasan jeruk nipis ke masakannya.


Nasinya itu intinya airnya yang ada di butir nasi minimal. Ada yang nasinya ditunggu umurnya sehari (atau nasi sisa sehari sebelumnya). Cara cepat : pake basmati atau long grain, atau nasi di microwave sampe keringnya cocok


Pake minyak yang banyak. Kalau pengen rasanya sama dengan warung,pake minyak yang banyak. Coba search di youtube nasi goreng warung,jangan kaget dengan jumlah minyak yang dipake.


Inti nasi goreng yang enak itu adalah minyak goreng yang diresapi (infused) dengan bawang putih (dan kemiri). Caranya bisa pake trik yang beredar di internet (minyak goreng + bawang putih + kemiri diblender/diuleg, kemudian ditinggal semalam di kulkas baru siap pakai),atau pake cara seperti memasak aglio lio. Yaitu minyak goreng + bawang putih dipotong setipis2nya + kemiri dipotong setipis2nya,kemudian dengan api paling kecil,sampe bawang putih berubah kuning.


Minyak goreng di atas itu bukan untuk menggoreng nasi,tapi untuk membaluti nasi dengan minyaknya. Ini filosofi wok stir fry.


Pake api sepanas-panasnya ketika nasi masuk (dan kecap),dan kecepatan tinggi (2 menit maksimal). Sebelum nasi masuk, semua bumbu sudah masuk. Jadi pinter2 ngiranya. Kalau kelamaan,nasinya melembek. Masih enak sih


Inti nasi goreng kayanya seperti itu. Bisa ditambah daging,dll. Bumbunya ya standard msg,merica,kaldu bubuk,garam.


Saya pake basmati,dan rasanya konsisten enak seperti di warung2.


Choc Chip Cookies

Created Sunday 29 October 2023


Here's the recipe, from <a href="http://hostthetoast.com/best-chewy-cafe-style-chocolate-chip-cookies/" title="http://hostthetoast.com/best-chewy-cafe-style-chocolate-chip-cookies/" class="http">http://hostthetoast.com/best-chewy-cafe-style-chocolate-chip-cookies/</a> (There's a video there as well)


INGREDIENTS
2 cups + 2 tablespoons all purpose flour
1 teaspoon baking soda
2 teaspoons cornstarch
1 ½ sticks (6 oz) butter, melted and cooled
1 teaspoon salt
1 cup dark brown sugar, lightly packed
½ cup granulated sugar
2 large eggs
2 teaspoons vanilla extract
1 1/2 cups dark chocolate chips, plus more for topping


INSTRUCTIONS
In a medium-sized bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, cornstarch, and salt. Set aside.
In a large bowl, beat together the cooled melted butter and the sugars with a hand-mixer for about one minute. Then, add in the eggs and vanilla extract. Beat until just combined.
Slowly add in the dry ingredients and mix briefly, just until there are no flour clumps left. Fold in the chocolate chips.
Cover and refrigerate the dough for 30 minutes to an hour.
Remove the dough from the refrigerator and preheat the oven to 350°F, making sure you have the racks in the middle of the oven. Line two baking sheets with parchment paper.
Scoop 1/4 cup of cookie dough at a time and roll into balls. Then, tear the balls in half by pulling gently on both sides. Smush the two halves together again, but this time have the lumpy, torn sides face upward. Place on the prepared baking sheet, making sure the cookies have plenty of space to spread. You should be able to fit 6-8 cookies on each tray.
Bake for about 12-14 minutes, rotating half-way through, or until the cookies have spread out and the edges are golden, but the centers of the cookies still look soft and undercooked. Every oven is different, so I recommend starting with just one or two cookies on the tray to see what baking time works best for you!
Let the cookies cool on the baking sheets until the cookies are firm enough to remove, about 15 minutes. As the cookies are cooling, press additional chocolate chips into the tops for a more bakery-style look.
Repeat with remaining batches, until all cookies are baked. Enjoy with a cold glass of milk!

How is it that the one percenters can continue to milk the cash cow with no thought for an end game? The business model of squeezing the lower classes is not sustainable. I know global business leaders don't lack vision. Do they just not care? Is acquisition of wealth a goal in itself? Why?


Resource rapine must be fun while it lasts. I wouldn't know. But without rebuilding the middle class to pump more money into the economy the resources are finite, and the under class won't be satisfied with bread and circuses forever. I saw a woman in a wheel chair hoist herself up on the side of a dumpster, legs dangling limply, so she could rummage for food, aluminum cans, etc. What kind of world, what kind of America is that?


Edit: this kind of blew up, and I'm grateful for the discussion. But to answer my own question, I think there's a few of things going on: gluttony, sociopathy, and functional fixedness.


Gluttony, because avarice seems to be an end onto itself, and because the "haves" control the corporations which are required by law to maximize profit for the shareholders.


Sociopathy because the "haves" got theirs and "devil take the hind most," however short sighted that is.


Functional fixedness, because it's easier to rape resources and milk the cash cow than it is to consider a triple bottom line, easier to care about and focus on the single metric of profit, rather than concern themselves with any long game of protecting the consumers of their products and services, the sources of their raw materials, or the environment, especially when it pushes up against their sole metric.


I in part blame government, not because it's not giving handouts to the "have nots" but because it has failed to adequately regulate business.


I partly blame business schools who have failed to adapt curriculum to a shifting world view, culture, and economy.


And I partly blame the media for reasons which should be obvious, RIP the fourth estate.


And I partly blame "the have nots" for letting it all happen, though really, we're just a frog in the pot set to boil.


People don’t like paying taxes - independent of status. However, in the past, there was an important reason to fully fund social programs - humans were useful. The industrialists needed literate and healthy workers and did not want to solely own the burden of providing education and health services for them. The state needed well trained and healthy soldiers and administrators. You also had a strong wave of socialism spreading through industrialized nations which incentivized capitalists to pony up a bit more for social programs. Today, industry and armies increasingly require far fewer individuals, leading to a dramatic increase in the number of “useless” humans. In the near future, with continued automation and development of ever smarter AIs, the number of people with no real use to society will grow dramatically. Combine this with the collapse of almost every socialist state, and you remove almost all incentive to fund state social programs. Since the wealthy see their taxes as largely paying for these services, and since they no longer benefit in the same way they used to, you see a desire on their part to dismantle state social programs and reduce their tax bill. The folly of course being when you have a dystopian society with a very few haves and a great many have-nots, the haves often times end up losing their heads.

model risiko audit = risiko inheren + kontrol + audit


ada psp osp sap key items representative samplingz


kalau risiko inheren rendah dan ada kontrol maka bukti yang diambil proporsi paling besar adalah tes kontrol lalu prosedur substantif primer dan prosedur substantif lainnya
kalau risiko inheren rendah namun tidak ada kontrol maka bukti yang diambil hanya dari prosedur substantif primer dan prosedur substantif lainnya
kalau risiko inheren tinggi dan ada kontrol maka bukti yang diambil seimbang antara tes kontrol, prosedur substantif primer, &amp; prosedur substantif lainnya
kalau risiko inheren tinggi dan tidak ada kontrol maka bukti yang diambil dari prosedur substantif primer dan prosedur substantif lainnya, namun mungkin komposisinya berbeda


terus ke minimum testing treshold dan timing procedure
kalau untuk yang minimal bisa 100-75 te, low 75-50 te, moderate 50-25 te, high 25-10 te ini untuk yang akun asset atau income
kalau untuk yang minimal bisa 50-25te, low 25-15 te, moderate 15-10 te, high 10-5te ini untuk yang akun liabilities atau expense


untuk timing procedure,
yang risikonya minimal bisa masa-masa awal periode pelaporan yaitu sampai 6 bulan
yang risikonya low (inheren besar tapi ada kontrol) bisa maksimal 3 bulan
yang lainnya semua sampai dengan 1 bulan sebelum tanggal neraca


untuk minimum sampling, kalo termasuk banyak biasanya 10%
kalau dibawah 50 minimum 5
kalau dibawah 5 biasanya 100%examination
